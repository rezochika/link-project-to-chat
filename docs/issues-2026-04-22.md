## Post-audit operational issues (resolved)

These were not in the original security/quality sweep but surfaced on 2026-04-22 when team delegations started failing. Both landed in commit `01f4645` (2026-04-23).

| # | Area | Issue | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | `bot.py` + `team_relay.py` | Partial-message relay: livestream edits during a long streaming reply caused `team_relay` to debounce-forward incomplete content; the first relayed chunk locked `_relayed_ids` so the final full text never reached the peer bot. | `_on_stream_event` now short-circuits in `group_mode`; team bots send once at finalize via `_send_to_chat`. |
| R2 | `team_relay.py` | Split-message fragmentation: Telegram's 4096-char cap turned one logical delegation into N separate NewMessage events, each spawning its own queued task in the peer bot (garbled parallel output). | New `(sender, reply_to_msg_id)` coalesce buffer with a 3s window; continuations without their own `@peer` mention are appended. |
| R3 | `personas/software_manager.md` | Manager persona had no brevity guard, so delegations routinely exceeded 4096 chars even when the spec was already written to a `docs/*.md` file. | Hard rule added: keep group messages under ~3000 chars; offload spec detail to `docs/` and post a short dispatch. |
